Bottom Line · Rev 3 · Actuals In · No Sugar

Recommendation: stay on Aloware and use this quote as a renegotiation lever. Only switch if call quality is the real pain.

The firm gap (annual, usage in)

  • 7 seats — Lean: Ringover ~$380/mo vs Aloware ~$490/mo → saves ~$110/mo (~$1.3K/yr). Parity flips it: Ringover ~$695/mo, pricier than Aloware.
  • 5 seats — Lean: Ringover ~$276/mo vs Aloware ~$353/mo → saves ~$77/mo (~$920/yr). Parity ~$501/mo, pricier.
  • Usage is a wash: ~$16–20/mo on both, confirmed from your invoices. It does not move the needle.

Why I'd hold

  • Best case is a sub-$1.5K/yr lean saving that reverses at feature parity — and only if you skip Empower and dodge per-user line fees.
  • Switching cost — number porting, 12-month lock-in, retraining, rebuilding Close + Bastion + MCP, A2P re-registration, productivity dip — almost certainly exceeds year-one savings.
  • You'd lose native MCP and trade free unlimited texting for a metered model.
  • The one real trigger: call quality. Ringover out-rates Aloware (4.6 vs 4.3) and quality is Aloware's #1 complaint. If your reps are hurting on call quality, that — not price — justifies a move.
